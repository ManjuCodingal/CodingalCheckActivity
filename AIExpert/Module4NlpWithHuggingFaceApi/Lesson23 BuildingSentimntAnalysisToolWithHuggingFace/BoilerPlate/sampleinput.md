# Duplicate Question Detector — Sample Inputs

This activity checks whether **two questions mean the same thing**, even if the words are different.

## Quick Start (Kids Version)
1. Run the program.
2. Choose:
   - `demo` → see ready-made examples
   - `custom` → type your own 2 questions
   - `exit` → stop

---

## Sample Inputs (Copy–Paste)

### 1) Password / Account
**Q1:** How do I reset my password?  
**Q2:** I forgot my password. How can I change it?

**Q1:** How do I delete my account?  
**Q2:** Can I remove my profile permanently?

---

### 2) Learning / Study
**Q1:** What is the best way to learn Python?  
**Q2:** How can I start learning Python easily?

**Q1:** How can I improve my math skills?  
**Q2:** What are tips to get better at math?

---

### 3) Places / Directions
**Q1:** Where is the nearest hospital?  
**Q2:** Which hospital is closest to me?

**Q1:** How do I get to the library?  
**Q2:** What’s the way to reach the library?

---

### 4) Time / Events
**Q1:** What time does the movie start?  
**Q2:** When does the film begin?

**Q1:** When does school reopen?  
**Q2:** What is the date school starts again?

---

### 5) Weather
**Q1:** Is it going to rain today?  
**Q2:** Will the weather be rainy today?

**Q1:** Will it be very hot tomorrow?  
**Q2:** Is tomorrow’s temperature going to be high?

---

## “Different Meaning” Examples (Not Duplicates)

**Q1:** How do I bake a chocolate cake?  
**Q2:** How do I fix a bicycle flat tire?

**Q1:** What is 2 + 2?  
**Q2:** What is the capital of India?

---

## Fun Challenge
Try making your own pairs:
- Ask the same question in **two different ways**
- Then ask **two totally different questions**
See if the detector agrees with you!
